01 · Executive Summary

The Case for Building This

India has one of the largest unmet mental health burdens in the world — over 197 million people suffer from some form of mental illness, yet fewer than 3% receive any care. The gap is not simply a shortage of professionals; it is a systems problem. People don't know where to start, can't identify whether they need professional help, don't know how to find credible therapists, and when they do, the entire experience — discovery, booking, payment, continuity — is fragmented and anxiety-inducing in itself.

Mindfully Yours is a platform that solves every step of that journey end-to-end: from a person's first acknowledgment that they are struggling, all the way through to sustained, quality therapeutic care — with an AI companion holding their hand throughout.

Core Thesis
The platform is not a directory of therapists. It is not a meditation app. It is not a chatbot. It is the only product in India that guides a user from emotional awareness all the way to sustained clinical care — with an intelligent AI companion acting as the thread between every touchpoint.

What This Document Covers

This proposal covers every technical decision, every product feature at micro-level, every infrastructure consideration, every user journey edge case, all compliance requirements, the phased delivery plan, risk analysis, and engagement terms. It is intended to be the single source of truth from which the PRD, design brief, and development brief will all derive.

03 · Product — Mental Health User (MHU)

The Complete MHU Experience

Every feature has been designed from the perspective of a first-time user who may be scared, embarrassed, or unsure whether they even need help. The experience must be warm, non-clinical in tone, non-judgmental in framing, and progressive in how it builds trust.

3.1 Onboarding & Registration

1
Anonymous Entry — Guest Mode

Users explore the app and interact with a limited AI companion before creating an account. Full registration is only required before booking a session or saving progress. Reduces friction for uncertain users. No sign-up wall

2
Registration — OTP + Email

Mobile number verified via OTP (MSG91). Email optionally linked for receipts and notifications. No mandatory personal details beyond phone number at sign-up. Name, age, gender, and location collected progressively as context is needed. bcrypt password hashing, JWT-based sessions, refresh token rotation. JWT + OTP

3
Preference Capture

Post-registration: 5-question preference screen capturing preferred language, preferred therapist gender, openness to online vs in-person sessions, and notification preferences. Stored as user preference vector in PostgreSQL, fed directly into the matchmaking algorithm.

4
AI Companion Introduction

First-time users are introduced to the AI companion — not as a chatbot, but as a named, consistent digital presence. The companion explains its role, asks a gentle opening question, and begins the triage process conversationally. No forms, no cold questionnaires.

3.7 Crisis Detection & Escalation System

Clinical Safety Requirement — Non-Negotiable
This system must be designed in consultation with Shreya before development begins. It is a clinical safety feature, not a product feature — it must meet clinical standards for crisis protocol, not just software standards for notifications.
1
Signal Detection

All AI companion messages, journal entries, and mood logs run through a real-time risk classification model. Flags for: suicidal ideation keywords, acute distress language, expressions of hopelessness, sudden sharp mood drops (≥4 points on 1–10 scale). Custom NLP classifier

2
Severity Assignment

Flagged signals assigned: Low (below-threshold but notable), Medium (concerning pattern), High (immediate risk language). Each level triggers a different protocol. High always triggers human escalation.

3
MHU-Facing Response

Companion shifts tone. SOS resources surfaced (iCall, Vandrevala Foundation, NIMHANS helplines). Option to call helpline directly from within the app. Option to trigger emergency alert to pre-registered trusted contact.

4
MHP Notification

If MHU has assigned MHP, a push notification and in-app alert is sent immediately with anonymised risk flag (severity level and timestamp only, no conversation content). MHP can acknowledge and reach out within the platform.

5
Admin Logging & Clinical Review

All crisis flag events logged in access-controlled audit table. Reviewed by clinical lead (Shreya) weekly. Pattern analysis used to improve classifier accuracy. No individual MHU data accessible to non-clinical admin staff.

04 · Product — Mental Health Professional (MHP)

The Complete MHP Experience

MHPs are the platform's supply side — their satisfaction, efficiency, and trust in the platform is as critical as MHU satisfaction. Every feature is designed to reduce administrative overhead so MHPs can focus entirely on clinical work.

4.1 MHP Onboarding & Verification

India-Specific Constraint
There is no automated API to verify RCI (Rehabilitation Council of India) or MCI (Medical Council of India) credentials in India. Verification is a manual process managed through the admin panel. MHPs are not visible to any MHU until verification status is "Active."
1
Profile Registration

MHP registers with: full name, email, mobile, professional registration number, qualifications, specialisations (multi-select from curated clinical list), languages spoken, years of experience, professional bio. Photo required. PostgreSQL

2
Document Upload

MHP uploads: degree certificates, registration certificate, government ID, additional certifications. Documents stored in S3 with restricted IAM access. Each document assigned status: pending → verified → active. S3 + IAM

3
Admin Verification Review

Admin team reviews documents via admin panel queue. Document-by-document approval or rejection with reason notes. MHP notified at each status change via email + push. Verification typically 48–72 hours. Clock shown in MHP dashboard.

4
Profile Activation & Setup

Once verified: MHP sets availability windows (recurring weekly + blocked dates), session rate per hour, maximum daily session cap, session format preferences, and payout bank account (Razorpay KYC). Profile goes live to MHUs.

06 · AI & Triage Engine

How the Intelligence Layer Works

The AI engine is the most technically complex and clinically sensitive component of the platform. Built correctly, it is also the biggest competitive moat — one that cannot be easily replicated. This section describes the full architecture of the AI layer.

Architecture Principle
The AI engine is NOT a general-purpose LLM wrapper. It is a structured system where a purpose-built orchestration layer controls what the LLM can and cannot say, what tools it can access, and how outputs are validated before reaching the user. Clinical safety requires this level of control.
LayerFunctionTechnology
Orchestration LayerControls conversation flow. Manages companion "modes" (casual check-in, triage, crisis, between-session support). Enforces guardrails — topics the companion must redirect (medical diagnosis, medication advice). Written as a NestJS state machine.NestJS State Machine
LLM IntegrationCalls to Claude Sonnet or GPT-4 via API for conversational generation. Every call includes: system prompt (role, guardrails, tone), conversation history (last N messages), retrieved memory context (RAG), and current emotional state vector.Anthropic API / OpenAI
Memory & RAGAfter each conversation, key information extracted and embedded. Stored in a vector database. Retrieved by semantic similarity at start of each new session. Enables cross-session memory without sending full conversation history to LLM.pgvector / Pinecone Embedding API
Triage ProtocolPHQ-9, GAD-7 questions distributed conversationally across multiple turns. Scoring calculated server-side, never exposed as a raw score to the user. Output maps to: wellbeing support (companion only) / mild clinical need / moderate-severe / crisis.Scoring Service Crisis Classifier
Output Safety LayerEvery LLM output checked before reaching user: no diagnostic language, no medication recommendations, no explicit descriptions of self-harm methods. Violations logged and used to improve system prompt guardrails iteratively.Safety Classifier Moderation API
Matchmaking AlgorithmTriggered after triage completion. Takes MHU profile vector (condition flags, language, location, preferences) and runs against MHP database using weighted cosine similarity. 6.1 Apple Guideline 1.4.2 Compliance

App Store Risk — Must Address at Design Stage
Apple's Guideline 1.4.2 requires mental health apps not to suggest, imply, or replace professional diagnosis. Violations cause App Store rejection and affect in-app language, AI output formatting, and onboarding copy — not just App Store metadata.
✓
Language Guardrails in AI Output All AI outputs use "self-reflection" or "check-in" framing — never diagnostic. "Based on what you've shared, you might benefit from speaking to a professional" vs "You appear to have moderate depression."
✓
Onboarding Disclosure First screen clearly states the companion is not a medical professional and does not provide diagnosis or medical advice. Always consult a qualified professional for medical concerns.
✓
App Store Metadata App Store description avoids diagnostic language. Category: "Health & Fitness" not "Medical." Privacy policy explicitly states data usage and AI/LLM involvement.
!
Clinical Review of AI Prompts All system prompts and guardrail language must be reviewed by Shreya before App Store submission. Any post-launch changes to AI behaviour require re-review by clinical lead.

9.2 RBAC Enforcement Rules

RBAC Must Be API-Level, Not Just Frontend
An MHP or MHU could construct raw API calls bypassing the frontend entirely. RBAC guards must be enforced in NestJS middleware on every API route — the frontend is a convenience layer, not a security boundary.
RuleEnforcement
MHU cannot access any other MHU's dataEvery query includes mandatory WHERE user_id = :authenticated_user_id. ORM-level scope guard applied globally to MHU entity queries.
MHP cannot access another MHP's patient recordsAll patient data access enforced via join through therapist-patient assignment table. Query returns empty set — not 403 — to prevent enumeration attacks.
MHP cannot access AI companion logs without MHU consentCompanion log access blocked at API route level unless a valid, timestamped consent record exists in the consent table for that MHU-MHP pair.
Admin cannot access individual clinical dataAdmin analytics queries use pre-aggregated anonymised views. Direct queries to clinical tables blocked for non-clinical admin roles at database role permission level, not just API guards.
All clinical data access is loggedEvery API request accessing clinical data logged to a separate append-only audit table: timestamp, user ID, role, resource accessed, action type. Audit table has no DELETE permission granted to any role.

10.2 Data Retention Policy

Active Users
Retained While Account Active

All clinical data, session history, companion logs, and mood data retained for the lifetime of an active account. User can delete individual entries at any time. Deletion is immediate and permanent — no soft deletes for clinical data.

Deleted Accounts
30-Day Deletion Window

On account deletion: immediate deactivation. PII purged within 30 days. Anonymised aggregate data retained indefinitely. Payment records retained 7 years for tax compliance — anonymised of PII.

Audit Logs
5-Year Retention

Audit logs retained for 5 years for regulatory compliance. Cannot be deleted by any user role — automated retention policy only. Stored in S3 Glacier after 1 year for cost efficiency.

Crisis Records
Clinical Retention Period

Crisis flag records retained per Shreya's clinical governance requirements. Minimum 5 years recommended for medico-legal protection. Anonymised for aggregate analysis. Access-controlled to clinical lead only.

12.1 DPDP Act Compliance

India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act (2023) applies fully to this platform. Mental health data is classified as sensitive personal data, requiring additional protections. Compliance must be built from Day 1 — retrofitting is both technically difficult and legally risky.

!
Explicit Consent at Every Touchpoint Granular consent for: data processing, AI companion usage, session recording, data sharing with MHP, analytics, and communications. Consent version tracked — re-consent triggered if consent text changes.
!
Data Localisation All data stored exclusively in AWS ap-south-1 (Mumbai). No cross-border data transfer. LLM API calls designed to minimise PII transmission — conversation summaries sent, not raw identifying data.
!
Right to Erasure Users can request full data deletion from within the app. Immediate deactivation, PII purge within 30 days, anonymised aggregate retention for lawful purposes. Deletion confirmation provided in writing.
!
Data Fiduciary Registration Mindfully Yours Pvt Ltd must register as a Significant Data Fiduciary with the Data Protection Board of India once the framework is fully notified. Legal counsel must track notification dates.
✓
Grievance Officer Designation A named Grievance Officer must be designated with contact displayed in-app and on the website. Complaints acknowledged within 72 hours, resolved within 30 days.

13 · Risk Register & Mitigations

Known Risks & How We Address Them

HIGH
AI Companion Clinical Safety Failure

Risk: AI companion produces harmful output — diagnostic language, inappropriate response to crisis signals, or factually wrong clinical information. Impact: direct harm to vulnerable user, legal liability, regulatory action.

Mitigation: Multi-layer output safety system (Section 6). All system prompts reviewed by Shreya. Fallback to human MHP contact triggered on any clinical uncertainty. Regular red-team testing of AI outputs. Safety classifier runs on every single LLM output.

HIGH
Data Breach of Clinical Records

Risk: Unauthorised access to session notes, companion logs, or assessment data. Impact: severe reputational damage, DPDP Act penalties, direct harm to users whose sensitive mental health data is exposed.

Mitigation: Field-level encryption on all clinical data. Zero-trust architecture. Annual penetration testing. Incident response plan documented and rehearsed before launch. All data access audit-logged.

HIGH
Apple App Store Rejection

Risk: App rejected under Guideline 1.4.2 for implying diagnosis or mental health claims. Impact: delayed launch, required rework, loss of iOS market.

Mitigation: Compliance addressed at design stage. Submission includes detailed reviewer notes explaining self-assessment framing. Legal counsel reviews all in-app language before submission.

MED
Razorpay Route KYC Delays

Risk: Razorpay Route KYC process takes 4–8 weeks. Impact: launch delay or inability to pay MHPs at launch.

Mitigation: Razorpay KYC initiated at Phase 1 start (Week 1). Fallback: manual bank transfers to MHPs while Route is being activated, with reconciliation in admin dashboard.

MED
MHP Supply Shortage at Launch

Risk: Insufficient verified MHPs onboarded before MHU launch creates poor matching or long wait times.

Mitigation: MHP onboarding and verification begins 6 weeks before MHU-facing launch. Target 30+ verified MHPs across key specialisations, languages, and cities before go-live. Admin tracking dashboard for pipeline visibility.

MED
Video Session Reliability in Tier 2/3 India

Risk: Third-party SDK has poor performance in cities with unreliable connectivity. Impact: poor session experience, MHP and MHU churn.

Mitigation: SDK chosen for India network performance track record. Adaptive bitrate + audio-only fallback built in. Post-session quality rating prompts to detect patterns. In-house WebRTC planned for Phase 5.

LOW
LLM Provider API Dependency

Risk: Primary LLM provider changes pricing, has outages, or modifies API in breaking ways.

Mitigation: LLM provider abstracted behind internal service layer — can switch providers without changes to companion logic. Secondary provider configured as fallback. Graceful degradation if API unavailable.
